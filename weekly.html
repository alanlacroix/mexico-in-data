The weekly

Mexico, once a week, in one email

Every Monday, the week's biggest moves in Mexico's economy, politics, and companies, in one email you can read in about five minutes. The lead stories are summarized from the source articles and linked. Under them, a board of the numbers that moved: the peso, inflation, the Banxico rate, remittances, and US–Mexico trade, each computed from official data. What's inside

  • Top of the week. The two or three stories that actually mattered, ranked and capped. A big week runs long; a quiet week runs short. It is never padded to fill space.
  • The board. The peso, headline and core inflation, the Banxico rate, remittances, and two-way US–Mexico trade. Each shows the latest value, the direction it moved, and the date it is from.
  • The rooms. Payments and fintech first, then companies and deals, economy, politics, and US–Mexico. A room appears only when something happened in it.
  • What to watch. The dated calendar ahead: rate decisions, CPI releases, USMCA milestones.

How it's made

The board is computed by code from wired official sources: Banxico, INEGI, and the US Census Bureau. The lead summaries are written only from the fetched article text of a trusted-source allowlist, never from memory, and every headline links to its origin. Tariff and trade items come straight from the US Federal Register in the government's own words.

One email a week. Every issue is reviewed by a person before it sends. Nothing goes out on autopilot.

The rest of the site

The weekly is the moving picture. The Briefing is the standing one: 40 sourced answers about Mexico's economy, security, and politics, refreshed every six hours. See the Economy room, the state-by-state Atlas, or the Sources page that lists every feed and when it last updated.
