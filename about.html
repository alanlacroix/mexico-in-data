About

Why I built this

I'm Alan Lacroix. I was born in Mexico City and moved to the United States when I was young. I'm building a business in Mexico now, and I go home often — and somewhere along the way I realized I'd lost touch with what was actually happening there: the economy, the politics, the mood of the place. This site is how I got it back.

What it is

The Mexico Brief pulls Mexico's numbers straight from the source: Banxico, INEGI, Hacienda, and the U.S. agencies that track the relationship. Numbers alone don't tell you what's going on, so I pair them with the developments that move them: policy changes, court rulings, and major deals, each linked to its report.

How it started

It began as a private tool, just for me — a way to stay current without wading through a dozen government portals in Spanish. Then family and friends started asking me to send it to them. So I opened it up. It's free, and I intend to keep it that way.

The weekly brief

Once a week I write a longer read: the key areas worth your attention across Mexico's economy, politics, and markets, compiled and explained in one place. It's built for investors, executives, and anyone with a real stake in the country and the region. See what's in it and subscribe →

A few things I hold to

  • Every figure links to the official source it came from, with the date it was published.
  • Most numbers come straight from Mexican official institutions — Banxico, INEGI, Hacienda. Where a figure is from a U.S. agency, a multilateral body (World Bank, IMF), a poll, or a market source, it's labeled as such — all of them listed on the Sources page.
  • When a feed goes quiet, its last value stays up, marked with the date it stopped.
  • The news is here to explain the numbers, not replace them. Nothing on this site is investment advice, and I have no affiliation with any government or party.

Tell me when I'm wrong

If a number looks wrong, or you know a source I should add, I want to hear it. I'm always looking for better data and clearer ways to frame what's happening.
