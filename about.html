About

Why I built this

My name is Alan Lacroix. I was born in Mexico City and moved to the United States when I was young. I'm building a business in Mexico now and I visit home often, and I realized I'd lost touch with the social, political, and economic reality of the country. So I built this to get it back.

What it is

It starts with as much real-time, first-party data as I can find: the official numbers, straight from the source. Numbers alone don't tell the whole story, so I pair them with the news that gives them context, always tied back to the data. It's the clearest way I've found to see what's actually going on.

How it started

It began as a way for me to stay up to date without wading through a dozen government portals in Spanish. Then family and friends found it useful, so I opened it up. It's free.

The weekly brief

Once a week I write a longer read that pulls together the key areas of interest in Mexico. It works well for investors, executives, or anyone interested in the country and the region. See what's in it and subscribe →

A few things I hold to

  • Every figure links to the source it came from, with the date it was published.
  • Mexican official institutions come first — Banxico, INEGI, Hacienda. U.S. agencies, multilateral bodies, polls, and market data are labeled as such, and listed on the Sources page.
  • When a feed goes quiet, its last value stays up, marked with the date it stopped.
  • The news is here to explain the numbers, not replace them. Nothing here is investment advice, and I have no affiliation with any government or party.

Tell me what I'm missing

I'm open to feedback, and always looking to add new sources, better data feeds, and clearer ways to frame the information. If a number looks wrong or you know a source I should add, please reach out.
